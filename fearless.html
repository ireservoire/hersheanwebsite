Fearless (Taylor's Version)

Fearless (Taylor's Version) is the first re-recorded album by American singer-songwriter Taylor Swift, released on April 9, 2021, via Republic Records. It is the re-recording of Swift's second studio album, Fearless (2008), a counter-measure by Swift following the 2019 dispute over the ownership of her back catalog released by Big Machine Records. It includes six previously unreleased "From the Vault" tracks.[a] Of the re-recorded tracks, 13 are from the original Fearless release, six are from the 2009 Platinum Edition, and one was a soundtrack single ("Today Was a Fairytale") for the 2010 film Valentine's Day.

Musically, Fearless (Taylor's Version) is a country pop album composed of guitars, banjo, fiddle, and strings. The lyrics are about Swift's adolescent reflections and feelings on love and heartbreak. Swift and Christopher Rowe produced all but the unreleased tracks to replicate the original recordings: they feature identical arrangements but with instruments and vocals that are more defined and controlled. Jack Antonoff and Aaron Dessner worked with Swift to produce the vault tracks, which have an indie- and-electronic-oriented soundscape brought by synthesizers and drum programming. Two vault tracks feature guest vocals from Maren Morris and Keith Urban respectively.

The album was preceded by "Love Story (Taylor's Version)", the re-recording of Fearless's lead single "Love Story", and two vault tracks, "You All Over Me" and "Mr. Perfectly Fine", all of which charted within the top 10 of Hot Country Songs. Fearless (Taylor's Version) topped the charts in English-speaking countries including the United States, where it became the first re-recorded album to reach number one on the Billboard 200 chart. Critics praised the re-recorded album for what they deemed an enhanced production quality that brings a nostalgic feel, but some felt the vault tracks were insubstantial. Many attributed Swift's enduring popularity to the lyrical sentiments that appeal to a broad audience, and the media highlighted Fearless (Taylor's Version) as a significant event contributing to a wider industry discourse over artists' rights.
